Edwin Arzeta: flower shop

June 8th - June 31st, 2026

Image Credit: Edwin Arzeta, Regardless of Time (2022) plastic flowers, Dior Homme glass bottle, PVA

beauty salon presents Edwin Arzeta: flower shop, an installation of drawings, paintings, and sculptures which draw upon the open-ended symbol of the flower and its deeply personal meanings to the artist. Using conventional materials, traditional bookbinding and paper folding techniques, artificial flowers, and various found objects, Arzeta has created works which are containers for memory, which engage with the flower as a marker of joy, achievement, grief, affection. Staged in a vacated flower shop in West Hollywood, the show is an exploration of the gendered charge of such spaces, an act of intervention which looks nostalgically towards the feminine. In the artist’s words:

‘When I think of flowers I think immediately of the glowing redness of canna lilies that as a young child, I would see in front of many apartment complexes in my Anaheim neighborhood. I think of the verdant honey lemon smell of the poisonous brugmansia that dangled like a yellow bell from an enormous tree in the same neighborhood. I think of a patch of large white calla lilies one block away, with their beautiful ombre of green stem to white spathe. My mother’s varied roses come to mind; roses that she would transplant from domicile to domicile - my favorites being the bright orange ones. My sister’s name was Violeta after the flower. She was the funniest person I ever knew. The vinca minors in our backyard in white and fuchsia would become little dolls that embodied the attitude of the heroines of fantasy stories as I pretended their wilted limp petals were hairdos.

While flowers are a symbol that belongs to the world history of sensorial language, for each of us they hold a range of meanings, from instant and shallow to deeply personal and tragic, even when reduced to the aerosols of a perfume mist. Whether from the floral section of the grocery store, the florist day-laborer, from our gardens, or from a webstore, the flower shop is the keeper of the flowers that we transact emotionally or cursorily – transactions to mark the passage of time.’
